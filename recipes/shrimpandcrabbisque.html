Shrimp, Crab, and Corn Bisque

Being from South Louisiana, seafood is a huge comfort food around here. This bisque is my go-to recipe on our few winter-like nights. This bisque is so rich and creamy, with just the right amount of spice to warm you up on cold days. It's also freezes nicely to reheat for the future!

Ingredients

  • 1 pound shrimp
  • 8-16 ounces lump blue crab meat
  • 1 can creamed corn
  • 1 can whole kernel corn, drained
  • 1 diced yellow onion
  • 1 bunch green onions
  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 3 cups chicken or shrimp stock
  • 2 cups half and half
  • 4 ounces cream cheese, softened
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 tablespoon onion powder
  • 2 tablespoons or to taste cajun spices
  • 2 teaspoons liquid crab boil

Steps:

  1. Melt butter in a dutch oven or heavy bottomed pot.
  2. Add in onions and saute until tender, reserve some green onions to garnish
  3. Sprinkle in flour and stir
  4. Add in spices and 1/4 of crab meat
  5. Pour in stock, stir, and bring to a gentle boil
  6. Add in cream cheese and stir until it melts
  7. Add in the corn
  8. Reduce heat and simmer for 10 mins
  9. Pour in the half & half
  10. Taste and adjust seasonings
  11. Add shrimp and crab boil to pot
  12. Simmer until shrimp are pink
  13. Gently add in remaining crab meat
  14. Simmer on low for 5-7 minutes
  15. Garnish with green onions, and serve
